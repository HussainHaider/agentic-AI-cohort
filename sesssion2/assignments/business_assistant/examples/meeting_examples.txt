================================================================================
MEETING SUMMARIZER — USAGE EXAMPLES
================================================================================
Feature: summarize_meeting()  /  MeetingSummarizer.summarize()

Scenario: Sarah's weekly leadership meeting produces raw, unstructured notes.
The assistant transforms them into clean, shareable summaries.
================================================================================


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXAMPLE 1 — Weekly Leadership Meeting
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Input:
  notes:
    "talked about q1 results, we hit 187k which is good, 15% above last quarter.
     sarah wants to push for enterprise clients more in q2.
     james said crm is outdated, needs upgrade by end of may.
     maria raised concern about customer churn going up slightly - from 2% to 3%.
     decided to do a customer success survey next week.
     john will prepare the investor deck for june board meeting.
     sarah to interview 2 senior eng candidates this week.
     next meeting june 1."

  date      : "May 15, 2026"
  attendees : "Sarah Mitchell, James Lee, Maria Gomez, John Park"

Expected output:
  ===========================================
  MEETING SUMMARY
  ===========================================
  Date: May 15, 2026
  Attendees: Sarah Mitchell, James Lee, Maria Gomez, John Park

  SUMMARY
  The leadership team reviewed Q1 2026 results (revenue $187K, +15% QoQ) and
  aligned on Q2 priorities. Key topics included CRM infrastructure, a small
  uptick in customer churn, and preparation for the June board meeting.

  KEY POINTS
  • Q1 revenue: $187,000 — 15% above Q4 2025
  • Q2 focus: accelerate enterprise client acquisition
  • CRM system upgrade required by end of May
  • Customer churn increased from 2% → 3%; customer success survey planned
  • June board meeting preparation underway

  DECISIONS
  1. Launch customer success survey next week to address churn uptick.
  2. Prioritise enterprise pipeline in Q2 go-to-market strategy.

  ACTION ITEMS
  • James: Upgrade CRM system by May 31, 2026
  • John: Prepare investor deck for June board meeting
  • Sarah: Interview two senior engineering candidates this week
  • Maria: Design and distribute customer success survey

  NEXT MEETING: June 1, 2026
  ===========================================


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXAMPLE 2 — Product Planning Session (no attendees specified)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Input:
  notes:
    "reviewed user feedback from last release. main complaints: slow dashboard
     load time and missing export to PDF feature. engineering estimates 2 weeks
     for PDF export, 1 sprint for performance fix. decided to prioritise perf fix
     in next sprint. pdf export goes into sprint after. design to create mockups
     for new analytics page by friday. no new features until existing backlog cleared."

  date      : "May 12, 2026"
  attendees : (none provided)

Expected output:
  ===========================================
  MEETING SUMMARY
  ===========================================
  Date: May 12, 2026
  Attendees: Not specified

  SUMMARY
  The product planning session focused on addressing user-reported issues from
  the last release. The team prioritised a performance fix ahead of the new
  PDF export feature and agreed to pause new feature development until the
  existing backlog is cleared.

  KEY POINTS
  • User feedback: slow dashboard load times and missing PDF export
  • Engineering estimate: 1 sprint for performance fix, 2 weeks for PDF export
  • New feature development paused until backlog is cleared

  DECISIONS
  1. Performance fix scheduled for the upcoming sprint.
  2. PDF export feature deferred to the following sprint.
  3. No new features until existing backlog is resolved.

  ACTION ITEMS
  • Design team: Create mockups for new analytics page by Friday
  • Engineering: Begin performance optimisation in next sprint

  NEXT MEETING: Not scheduled
  ===========================================


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HOW TO ENTER MULTI-LINE NOTES (interactive mode)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When using run() interactively, type your notes line by line and type END
on a new line when finished:

  Enter your meeting notes below.
  When done, type 'END' on a new line and press Enter.

  > Discussed Q1 revenue: $187k, up 15%
  > Sarah to hire two engineers
  > Next meeting June 1
  > END


================================================================================
QUICK REFERENCE — API CALL
================================================================================

  from business_assistant.features.meeting_summarizer import MeetingSummarizer

  summarizer = MeetingSummarizer()

  notes = """
  Reviewed Q1 results. Revenue $187k, +15% QoQ.
  James to upgrade CRM by May 31.
  Next meeting June 1.
  """

  # Direct API call
  summary = summarizer.summarize(
      notes=notes,
      date="May 15, 2026",
      attendees="Sarah, James, Maria"   # optional
  )
  print(summary)

  # Interactive (prompts user for notes and date)
  summarizer.run()
